STAR Assessment Feedback

Practice Session: Union Negotiations & Labor Relations

Overall Readiness Almost Ready

STAR Component Breakdown

S
Situation Strong

Set a clear scene with specific context including role, union involvement, and potential strike, allowing visualization of the scenario.

"This was a nursing union. I was a chief medical officer and the nurses were talking about going on strike."

T
Task Strong

Clearly explained personal responsibility on the administrative side to navigate the difficult situation.

"I was expected to be on the administrative portion and figure out what was going on and how to navigate such a difficult situation."

A
Action Adequate

Improved by focusing on personal actions using "I" and providing specifics like meetings and agendas, but lacked sequence and fuller detail.

"I had routine medical staff meetings and organized these with specific agenda items."

Expand into 2-3 sequential steps you took, e.g., "First, I scheduled one-on-one meetings with key union reps, then I facilitated joint agenda-setting sessions."

R
Result Needs Work

No results or outcomes were mentioned, missing impact or learning.

Always end with quantifiable impact or lesson, e.g., "This prevented the strike, improving staff morale by 20% based on follow-up surveys, and I learned the value of early transparency."

Key Strengths

Chose a highly relevant example directly addressing union negotiations, aligning with the skill gap.

"I was actually involved in union discussions once the scene was, you know, this was a nursing union."

Showed responsiveness to feedback by refining actions with "I" statements.

"I did. I had routine medical staff meetings and organized these with specific agenda items."

Priority Improvements

Response was incomplete and cut off, lacking a full STAR structure.

Practice delivering a complete 1-2 minute response without pausing, timing yourself to ensure all parts are covered.

Try: "To wrap up: As a result, we averted the strike and built stronger labor relations."

No quantification or specific outcomes shared.

Brainstorm metrics from the experience (e.g., number of meetings, averted costs, feedback scores) before practicing.

Try: "The outcome was resolving 80% of concerns without escalation, saving the hospital an estimated $50K in potential strike costs."

Next Steps

Session Progress

Clear improvement in the Action section—from vague mentions to specific "I" focused steps like organizing meetings after coach feedback.

In your next practice, focus on completing full STAR responses with quantified Results, using a timer for 1-2 minutes. Rehearse this union example end-to-end to build confidence.
